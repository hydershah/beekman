Important Regulatory Information

These disclosures contain important information about Beekman Strategic LLC, our services, fees, risks, and regulatory status. Please read them carefully and contact us with any questions.

1. Firm Registration & Regulatory Status

1.1 United States

Beekman Strategic LLC is a financial advisory firm headquartered in Boca Raton, Florida. Our regulatory status and affiliations:

  • Investment Adviser: Beekman Strategic provides investment advisory services. Our Form ADV is available upon request and through the SEC's Investment Adviser Public Disclosure (IAPD) website at adviserinfo.sec.gov
  • Broker-Dealer Status: Beekman Strategic is NOT a registered broker-dealer. Securities transactions are conducted through qualified third-party broker-dealers
  • State Registrations: We are registered or notice-filed in jurisdictions where required based on client locations

1.2 International Operations

Our global partner network operates under various regulatory frameworks:

  • United Kingdom: Activities conducted through FCA-authorized partners
  • Switzerland: Services provided through FINMA-regulated entities
  • Singapore: Activities conducted through MAS-licensed partners
  • Dubai: Services provided through DFSA-authorized entities
  • Luxembourg: Operations conducted through CSSF-regulated entities
  • Monaco: Services provided through locally authorized partners

Global Network

Beekman Strategic operates through a network of 35+ global issuers and legal partners across 8 financial centers. Each partner maintains appropriate licenses and registrations in their respective jurisdictions.

2. Form ADV Information

2.1 ADV Part 2A - Firm Brochure

Our Form ADV Part 2A contains important information about:

  • Types of advisory services offered

  • Disciplinary history (if any)
  • Other financial industry activities and affiliations
  • Code of Ethics summary
  • Brokerage practices and best execution

You may obtain our Form ADV by:

We will provide our Form ADV to prospective clients before or at the time of engagement.

3. Fee Disclosures

3.1 Advisory Fees

Our fee structures vary based on the services provided:

  • Asset Management: Annual fees based on assets under management, typically ranging from 0.50% to 1.50% depending on asset size and complexity
  • Investment Banking: Transaction-based fees, success fees, and retainers as agreed in engagement letters
  • Structuring Services: Project-based fees for fund formation, SPV creation, and vehicle structuring
  • Consulting: Hourly or project-based fees for specific advisory engagements

3.2 Additional Costs

In addition to our advisory fees, clients may incur:

  • Custodian Fees: Charged by third-party custodians for safekeeping assets
  • Administrator Fees: For fund administration services
  • Legal Fees: Third-party legal counsel for documentation
  • Regulatory Filing Fees: Government and regulatory filing costs
  • Trading Costs: Brokerage commissions and transaction fees
  • Issuer Costs: Fees charged by product issuers for structured products

3.3 Fee Negotiability

Fees are generally negotiable based on the scope of services, relationship size, and complexity. All fees will be clearly disclosed in your engagement agreement before services commence.

4. Risk Disclosures

Investment Risk Warning

ALL INVESTMENTS INVOLVE RISK, INCLUDING THE POTENTIAL LOSS OF PRINCIPAL. Past performance is not indicative of future results. There is no guarantee that any investment strategy will achieve its objectives.

4.1 General Investment Risks

  • Market Risk: Investments may lose value due to economic, political, or market conditions
  • Principal Risk: You may lose some or all of your invested capital
  • Interest Rate Risk: Changes in interest rates can affect investment values
  • Currency Risk: International investments are subject to exchange rate fluctuations
  • Inflation Risk: Returns may not keep pace with inflation
  • Liquidity Risk: Some investments may be difficult to sell when desired
  • Credit Risk: Issuers may default on obligations

4.2 Structured Product Risks

  • Complexity Risk: Structured products may be difficult to understand and value
  • Liquidity Risk: May be difficult or impossible to sell before maturity
  • Issuer Credit Risk: Returns depend on the creditworthiness of the issuer
  • Principal Protection Limitations: Protection features may have conditions or caps
  • Early Redemption: Selling before maturity may result in losses
  • Tax Uncertainty: Tax treatment may be complex or uncertain

4.3 Cross-Border Risks

  • Multi-Jurisdiction Exposure: Transactions may be subject to laws of multiple countries
  • Regulatory Changes: Laws and regulations may change unexpectedly
  • Political Risk: Political instability may affect investments
  • Tax Treaty Risk: Tax treatment under treaties may change

4.4 Technology & AI Risks

Our use of Beekman AI involves:

  • AI-assisted analysis has inherent limitations
  • Models are based on historical data that may not predict future outcomes
  • Technology systems may experience failures or errors
  • Cybersecurity risks exist with any technology platform

5. Conflicts of Interest

5.1 Potential Conflicts

We are committed to transparency about potential conflicts:

  • Fee-Based Compensation: Our fee structures may create incentives that differ from yours
  • Issuer Relationships: We have relationships with product issuers who may compensate us
  • Referral Arrangements: We may receive compensation for client referrals
  • Affiliated Transactions: Some transactions may involve affiliated entities
  • Multiple Client Representation: We may represent multiple clients with potentially conflicting interests

5.2 Conflict Management

We manage conflicts through:

  • Full disclosure of material conflicts
  • Written policies and procedures
  • Client consent where required
  • Fair allocation procedures
  • Compliance monitoring and oversight

6. Custody & Brokerage

6.1 Custody Practices

  • Client assets are held by qualified third-party custodians
  • Beekman Strategic does not take physical custody of client funds or securities
  • Clients receive account statements directly from custodians
  • We encourage clients to compare our reports with custodian statements

6.2 Best Execution

We are committed to seeking best execution for client transactions by considering:

  • Price and commission costs
  • Speed and likelihood of execution
  • Size and nature of the order
  • Confidentiality and market impact
  • Quality of research and other services

7. Client Eligibility Requirements

7.1 Investor Qualifications

Our services are designed for sophisticated investors:

  • Ultra-High-Net-Worth: Generally clients with $30M+ in investable assets
  • Accredited Investors: As defined by SEC Regulation D for certain offerings
  • Qualified Purchasers: As required for certain fund investments
  • Sophisticated Investors: Those with experience in complex financial transactions

7.2 Geographic Restrictions

  • Services may not be available in all jurisdictions
  • Certain products are restricted to specific investor categories
  • We do not provide services to persons in sanctioned countries
  • We comply with OFAC, EU, and other sanctions programs

8. Business Continuity

Beekman Strategic maintains a Business Continuity Plan designed to:

  • Ensure continued operations during significant business disruptions
  • Protect client data and assets
  • Maintain communication with clients
  • Provide alternative means of accessing services

In the event of a significant disruption, clients may contact us through alternative channels that will be communicated as needed.

Beekman Strategic is committed to:
